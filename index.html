Chester Bennington

The man who inspired the world with music

Life of a depressed superstar

Early Life

Chester Bennington was born to Lee Russell Bennington on March 20th, 1976. He developed an interest in music at an early age. He was greatly inspired by the bands, Stone Temple Pilots and Depeche Mode.

Chester's bad days took over when he turned seven years old. An older male friend of his used to touch him inappropriately and this molestation became a full-blown violent sexual assault. At age 11, his parents split which further scarred Chester. He began to remain quiet and his behavior changed drastically after his parents' divorce.

Chester was also bullied at school for looking different and being skinny. He used to stay alone and han no friends during his tenure at the school. Young Chester, depressed and alone, found his peace in music and started to take music more seriously. He found his niche where he could be more recognized as someone other than a bullied and sad child.

Drug Addiction and Alcoholism

Chester Bennington struggled with drug addiction and alcoholism from a very young age. He started doing drugs like meth, marijuana, cocaine, and LSD when he was 13-years old.

Susan found out in no time that her son was on hard drugs. She worried about Chesters physical and mental health. Therefore, she curfewed 17-year-old Chester from going out. Chester started working at burger king to make a living.

"I was on 11 hits of acid a day. I dropped so much acid I'm surprised I can still speak! [...]
I used pot to get off drugs. Every time I'd get a craving I'd smoke my pot.""
-Chester Bennington, in a Kerrang interview [2016]

Musical Career

Chester's musical career began in 1993 with his friend, Sean Dowell. Later in 1993, Chester got an opportunity to become the lead vocalist of the band “Grey Daze”. He performed for five years for the band until 1998, when he left the band.

In 1998, Chester met the vice president of Zomba Music, Jeff Blue. Jeff recognized Chester's work and asked him to audition for a boy band named Xero (later called Linkin Park).Chester cleared the audition and was liked by other members. He moved to Los Angeles in 1999 to work with Xero. The band later changed its name to Hybrid Theory. In 2000, they released an album named Hybrid Theory that remains their most impactful album.

The making of Linkin Park

Chester suggested the band change its name to Lincoln Park. However, the domain was already occupied, so the band was finally named 'Linkin Park', as we know of it today.

They released amazing award winning metal tracks. Songs like Numb, In the end, Meteora, Minutes to Midnight, A Thousand Suns, and One More Light are chartbuster tracks of Linkin Park. Chester often told in interviews that his music was closely related to his personal life.

Linkin Park bagged two Grammy awards, two Billboard Music Awards, six American Music Awards, and four MTV Video Music Awards. Linkin Park became famous as the sixth greatest band of the music era.

Personal Life and Children

Bennington was in a love affair with Elka Brand for few years. Bennington had a son named Jamie with Elka and later adopted a child as well. Bennington and Elka broke up in 1996 when Bennington met his second wife, Samantha Marie Olit.

They married in the same year. Samantha gave birth to Bennington's son, Draven Sebastian Bennington. However, the marriage did not last. The couple broke up in 2005 due to increasing tension between both.

A few years later, Bennington married Talinda Ann Bentley, a former Playboy magazine model. The couple had three babies together. Bennington had six children in total.

Suicide

On May 18th, 2017, Chester's closest friend Chris Cornell was found dead in his Detroit apartment. Cornell suffered from depression and committed suicide by hanging himself. Cornell's death took a toll on Chester's mental health. He could not make peace with his closest friend's demise.

Chester commented on Cornell's death,

“I can't imagine a world without you in it. I pray you to find peace in the next life.”

Almost a month after his friend's tragic death, Chester decided to end his life as well. Chester could not take the mental pressure anymore.

On July 20th 2017, Chester Bennington took his life by hanging himself in his house in California, leaving behind six children and wife Talinda.
